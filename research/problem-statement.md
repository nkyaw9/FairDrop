# Problem Statement

How might we design a more transparent and trustworthy online drop experience that gives real buyers a fair chance to access high-demand products such as concert tickets, sneakers, collectibles, and limited-release items before they are dominated by bots and resellers?

## Background

Current online drop systems often use waiting rooms, queues, CAPTCHA verification, purchase limits, and bot detection systems. However, many users still feel frustrated by the experience and believe the process is unclear or unfair. High-demand products frequently sell out within seconds and later reappear on resale platforms at heavily inflated prices.

This issue affects multiple industries including:
- Concert tickets
- Sneakers
- Collectibles and figures
- Gaming consoles
- Trading cards
- Limited-edition merchandise

Many users feel they are competing against bots, resellers, faster internet connections, or hidden queue algorithms rather than having a truly fair opportunity to purchase products at retail price.

## Goal

The goal of this project is to explore whether a more transparent, trustworthy, and fairness-focused drop infrastructure system could improve the user experience for high-demand online releases while balancing technical scalability and anti-bot protection.
